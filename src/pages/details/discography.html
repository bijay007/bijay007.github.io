With this album, the band became a rock band. Since then it changed its sound to folk-rock. The album was recorded with live sessions where all musicians played their parts, contrary to the previous productions when session players assisted them. The title song Resham, Lampate Surati, and Mai Nache remain inseparable parts of the setlist when the band performs. This album is credited for introducing, exploring, and popularizing the folk-rock genre in the Nepali musical scene.

The Album and the music within folk melodies from the Nepali hills have been the 'lifeforce' of Nepathya's music. Nepathya has always respected local essence and worked with such complex issues in its own unique style. Their style of collecting songs and melodies from across the Nepali landscape has recently been documented in the award documentary film- Bhedako Oon jasto ....in search of a song' which has portrayed the search for this title song.

Nepathya is the Foremost folk-rock band of Nepal consistently popular since 1990, which has well maintained its social outreach through Lyrics and performance. Nepathya has been mobilizing thousands of people through their songs and touring concerts by initiating collective social action, starting from the basic theme- peace and security in a country facing the most challenging process of our times, a peaceful political stability. During insurgency, Nepathya was the only artistic group to travel intensively across the nation advocating for peace with their concert tours. The style of songs and the nature of the tours initiated since 2001 till date are testimony to Nepathya's goal for social change through music.
